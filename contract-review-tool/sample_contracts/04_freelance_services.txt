INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Independent Contractor Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of April 1, 2025, between:

CLIENT: Redline Productions LLC, a California LLC ("Client")
CONTRACTOR: Devon Michaels, an individual ("Contractor")

1. SERVICES
Contractor agrees to provide the following services ("Services"):
- Screenplay coverage and script analysis reports
- Script doctoring and dialogue revisions as requested
- Development notes on up to 10 projects per month
- Attendance at weekly development meetings (virtual)

2. TERM
This Agreement commences April 1, 2025, and continues on a month-to-month basis until terminated by either party upon 14 days' written notice.

3. COMPENSATION
3.1 Rate. Contractor shall be compensated at $85 per hour for all Services rendered.

3.2 Invoicing. Contractor shall submit invoices on the 1st and 15th of each month. Client shall pay undisputed invoices within 45 days of receipt.

3.3 Disputed Invoices. Client may dispute any invoice item within 10 days of receipt. Client shall pay the undisputed portion while dispute is pending.

3.4 Late Payment. Invoices unpaid after 45 days shall accrue interest at 1.5% per month.

3.5 Expenses. Pre-approved expenses will be reimbursed within 30 days of submission with receipts.

4. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
4.1 Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee of Client. Contractor shall be solely responsible for all taxes, insurance, and benefits. Contractor shall have no authority to bind Client in any agreement.

4.2 Contractor retains the right to perform services for other clients during the term of this Agreement, provided such work does not create a conflict of interest with Client's projects.

4.3 Contractor shall provide all tools and equipment necessary to perform the Services unless otherwise agreed in writing.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
5.1 Work for Hire. All deliverables, reports, scripts, revisions, and materials created by Contractor in connection with the Services ("Work Product") shall be considered works made for hire owned by Client.

5.2 Assignment. To the extent any Work Product is not a work made for hire by operation of law, Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns all rights, title, and interest therein to Client, including all copyrights and derivative rights.

5.3 Pre-Existing IP. Contractor retains all rights to pre-existing intellectual property ("Background IP"). Contractor grants Client a non-exclusive license to use Background IP incorporated in Work Product solely for Client's business purposes.

5.4 Portfolio Rights. Contractor may list the general nature of work performed for Client in a professional portfolio but may not disclose project titles, content, or details without Client's written consent.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY
Contractor shall not disclose any information relating to Client's projects, business operations, or clients, during the term of this Agreement and for 3 years thereafter.

7. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES
Contractor represents and warrants that: (a) Contractor has the right to enter into this Agreement; (b) the Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner; (c) Work Product will be original and will not infringe any third-party rights; and (d) Contractor is not subject to any non-compete or other restriction that would prohibit performance of the Services.

8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
Client's liability to Contractor shall not exceed the total fees paid in the 3-month period preceding the claim. Neither party shall be liable for consequential, indirect, or special damages.

9. TERMINATION
9.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement upon 14 days' written notice.

9.2 Client may terminate immediately for cause, including Contractor's material breach, criminal conduct, or failure to deliver work meeting professional standards.

9.3 Upon termination, Client shall pay for all Services rendered and accepted prior to the termination date. No kill fee or compensation for anticipated future work shall be owed.

10. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Any dispute shall first be subject to good-faith negotiation for 30 days. If unresolved, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration in Los Angeles, California. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

11. GOVERNING LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

12. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This Agreement, together with any statements of work attached hereto, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties regarding the subject matter hereof.
