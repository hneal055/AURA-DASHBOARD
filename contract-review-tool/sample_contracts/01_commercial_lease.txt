COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Commercial Lease Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 1, 2025, by and between:

LANDLORD: Meridian Properties LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, with its principal office at 4800 Wilshire Boulevard, Suite 200, Los Angeles, CA 90010 ("Landlord")

TENANT: Silverline Productions Inc., a California corporation, with its principal office at 1200 Cahuenga Avenue, Hollywood, CA 90028 ("Tenant")

1. PREMISES
Landlord hereby leases to Tenant the commercial space located at 1200 Cahuenga Avenue, Suite 400, Hollywood, CA 90028, consisting of approximately 3,200 square feet of office space ("Premises").

2. TERM
The lease term shall commence on February 1, 2025 ("Commencement Date") and shall expire on January 31, 2028 ("Expiration Date"), unless sooner terminated in accordance with the terms hereof. Tenant shall have no right to renew or extend this Lease except as expressly set forth herein.

3. RENT
3.1 Base Rent. Tenant shall pay monthly base rent as follows:
- Year 1 (Feb 2025 – Jan 2026): $9,600/month ($3.00/sq ft)
- Year 2 (Feb 2026 – Jan 2027): $10,240/month ($3.20/sq ft)
- Year 3 (Feb 2027 – Jan 2028): $10,880/month ($3.40/sq ft)

3.2 Payment. Rent is due on the 1st of each month. Rent received after the 5th shall incur a late fee of 10% of the monthly rent amount. Rent must be paid by wire transfer or certified check only. Cash payments will not be accepted.

3.3 Security Deposit. Upon execution, Tenant shall deposit $19,200 (two months' rent at Year 1 rate) as a security deposit. Landlord may apply this deposit toward any unpaid rent or damages without prior notice to Tenant. Landlord shall have 60 days after lease expiration to return the remaining deposit, if any.

4. USE OF PREMISES
The Premises shall be used solely for general office and production administrative purposes. Tenant shall not use the Premises for filming, manufacturing, retail, or any purpose that generates foot traffic from the general public without Landlord's prior written consent, which may be withheld in Landlord's sole and absolute discretion.

5. OPERATING EXPENSES
In addition to Base Rent, Tenant shall pay 100% of all increases in operating expenses above the base year (2024) operating expenses, including but not limited to property taxes, insurance, utilities, maintenance, and management fees. Landlord's determination of operating expenses shall be final and binding on Tenant.

6. ALTERATIONS
Tenant shall make no alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises without Landlord's prior written consent. All approved alterations shall become Landlord's property upon installation and may not be removed by Tenant. Landlord may, at its sole discretion, require Tenant to restore the Premises to original condition at Tenant's expense upon lease expiration.

7. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
Tenant accepts the Premises in "as-is" condition. Tenant shall be solely responsible for all interior repairs and maintenance. Landlord shall maintain only the building's structural elements and common areas. HVAC maintenance and replacement, if needed, shall be Tenant's responsibility.

8. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING
Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet all or any portion of the Premises without Landlord's prior written consent, which Landlord may withhold for any reason. Any attempted assignment without consent shall be void and shall constitute a material default.

9. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES
9.1 Default. Tenant shall be in default if: (a) Tenant fails to pay rent within 3 days of written notice; (b) Tenant breaches any other covenant and fails to cure within 5 days of notice; (c) Tenant becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy.

9.2 Remedies. Upon default, Landlord may immediately terminate this Lease and pursue all remedies available at law or equity, including acceleration of all remaining rent for the full lease term.

10. INDEMNIFICATION
Tenant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Landlord and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from any and all claims, losses, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from Tenant's use of the Premises or any breach of this Lease.

11. HOLDOVER
If Tenant remains in possession after the Expiration Date without Landlord's written consent, such holdover shall constitute a month-to-month tenancy at 200% of the last monthly rent rate, and Tenant shall be liable for any damages Landlord suffers as a result of such holdover.

12. GOVERNING LAW
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California. Any dispute shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Los Angeles County. THE PREVAILING PARTY IN ANY DISPUTE SHALL NOT BE ENTITLED TO RECOVER ATTORNEYS' FEES.

13. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. No modification shall be binding unless in writing and signed by both parties.
